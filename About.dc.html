Since 2018 · Halsey Quarter

A bakery built on one stubborn idea.

CAFÉ started as a two-burner rented kitchen and a market stall that sold out by nine. The idea then is the idea now: make a small number of things properly, from real ingredients, and don't cut the corners nobody sees. Butter is butter. Dough is given the time it asks for.

We took the corner of Vine and Halsey in 2020 — a narrow room with a good oven and better light. The lamination bench sits where you can see it. Mornings start at four; the first croissants hit the counter at six, and we bake in waves until the flour runs low, which it always does.

Nothing here is scaled for volume. The menu shifts with the season and the market, the bread is proofed overnight, and the people who bake it are the same people who hand it to you. If we sell out early, it's because we would rather run short than bake ahead.

How we work

Slow on purpose.

Good pastry can't be hurried and we've stopped trying. Doughs rest cold for days, bread ferments overnight, and coffee is dialled in fresh each morning. It means we make less — but everything that leaves the counter is worth the wait.
